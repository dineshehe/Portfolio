About Me

I was the second topper in my 10th grade, especially strong in Science. Even
then, I chose the Arts stream often unfairly seen by society as a choice for those who give up not because I had to, but because I had a dream:
to crack the UPSC. Many people questioned my decision, but I followed my own path and completed my 12th with 93%.
Just as I finished school, the world came to a halt during the COVID-19 pandemic. The lockdown kept me at home for two years, and during that time, life tested me in unexpected ways. My mother's health became critical due to heart failure, and those years were spent going back and forth to hospitals. Somewhere along the way, my study routine faded, but my sense of responsibility grew stronger.
When things slowly returned to normal, I restarted my UPSC preparation. With time, I realized that this journey demands not just hard work, but patience beyond measure. I appeared for a few one-day exams and even cleared some, but I chose not to join due to distant postings. By then, two years had passed, and I began to feel the need to become financially independent.
That's when life quietly introduced me to something new. While reading current affairs one day, I came across a website that fascinated me. A simple thought crossed my mind how are these websites even built? And that small question became the beginning of something big.
I started exploring coding through YouTube, learning little by little in my free time. Slowly, curiosity turned into interest, and interest turned into passion. When I realized how vast and promising the tech world is, I decided to make it my Plan B which, over time, started feeling like my true calling.
To take things seriously, I joined a professional web development course at
Ducat Gurgaon. There, I began with HTML and CSS and gradually learned programming languages, databases, AI tools, chatbot integration, and modern frameworks. I never felt bored in fact, every day felt like building something new, something meaningful.
I also took help from AI while learning, and even while building this portfolio. It helped me move faster, but the effort and curiosity have always been mine.
Today, I have built multiple websites, each one giving me confidence and joy. Now, I no longer fear the question “What if UPSC doesn't work out?” Because I have found something I can do for a lifetime, without ever getting tired of it.
Coding is no longer just a backup plan for me it is a journey I truly enjoy. I have learned a lot, but I know there is still so much more ahead. And I am ready for it.
All I ask for is a little support from above. The effort will always be mine and the blessings, I leave to God.

Dinesh Sharma
